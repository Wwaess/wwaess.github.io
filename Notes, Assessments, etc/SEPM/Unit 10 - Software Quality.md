# What is this article about?

Verdugo et al (2024) is less about developing models to represent software quality, and more about how researching software quality can be translated into effective long-term real-world practice. The authors describe how AQCLab evolved from a research initiative to an accreditted laboratory which assesses quality characteristics like maintainability, functionality, reliability and security, going on to explain how the models they used could be reverse-engineered into reliable indicators for perceived quality. In particular, the paper expored how these models followed ISO standards and how they as measurable indicators could result in repeatable, trustworthy quality-assessments. 


The QualCode article (Azhir et al., 2025) is a research paper aiming to show how a new framework "QualCode" via machine-learning assistance could be used to predict the maintainability of software - part of the ISO/IEC 25010, a collection of quality characteristics that should be tested when evaluating a product's quality.
